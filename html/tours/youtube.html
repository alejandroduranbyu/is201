The Smiley Face Was Invented for Corporate America
In 1963, a graphic artist named Harvey Ball was paid $45 to design a morale-boosting symbol for an insurance company. He never trademarked it. What happened next made it one of the most reproduced images in history.

How the Smiley Face Was Created — World Smile Day
From Harvey Ball's original design to World Smile Day, this short documentary traces how a simple yellow circle became a global symbol of goodwill — and a corporate battleground.
